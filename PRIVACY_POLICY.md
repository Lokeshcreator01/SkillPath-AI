## Information Collection and Use

For a better experience, while using our Service, we may require you to provide us with certain personally identifiable information to enable cloud storage integration.

### 1. Cloud Storage Services
MoonDrive integrates with third-party cloud storage providers to manage your files. We communicate directly with these services using their official APIs. We do **not** store your login credentials (passwords) on our servers; authentication is handled via secure OAuth 2.0 protocols.

*   **Google Drive:** We use the Google Drive API to list, upload, download, and delete files as requested by you.
    *   **Google User Data:** We access your Google Drive files only when you explicitly log in and grant permission. This data is transferred directly between your device and Google's servers. We do not sell or share this data with third parties.
*   **Microsoft OneDrive:** We use the Microsoft Graph API to manage your OneDrive files.
*   **Dropbox:** We use the Dropbox API to manage your Dropbox files.

### 2. File Access (Local & Cloud)
To function as a file manager, MoonDrive requires permission to access the storage on your device (`READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE`, `WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE` or `MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE`).
*   We use this permission to display, move, copy, delete, and upload files from your device to your connected cloud accounts.
*   Downloaded files are stored in your device's local storage/cache.

### 3. Application Data
*   **Log Data:** In case of an error in the app, we collect data and information (through third-party products) on your phone called Log Data. This may include your device Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of the app when utilizing our Service, the time and date of your use of the Service, and other statistics.

We value your trust in providing us your Personal Information, thus we are striving to use commercially acceptable means of protecting it. But remember that no method of transmission over the internet, or method of electronic storage is 100% secure and reliable, and we cannot guarantee its absolute security.
*   OAuth tokens used to access your cloud accounts are stored securely on your device using strictly protected storage mechanisms (Shared Preferences/Keystore).
*   We do not have access to your account passwords.
